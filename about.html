As a biologist, I am interested in the crosstalk between diet and human gut microbiome and how they impact health and disease. I received my PhD in Computational Biology from Duke University in the Lawrence David lab and I am currently a postdoctoral fellow in the Robert Britton lab at the Baylor College of Medicine in Houston, Texas. My current projects include identifying metabolic traits that may have contributed to the emergence of outbreak variants of Clostridioides difficile, understanding how the gut microbiome responds to dietary sugars, and leveraging machine learning approaches for analysis of high-throughput experiments. I received my PhD in Computational Biology and Bioinformatics from Duke University in the Lawrence David lab.
